Limit Setup


This guide provides step-by-step instructions on how to set up limits for a customer within a financial institution. Limits are the sanctioned amount given by the financial institution to the organization. It covers creating limits, entering necessary details such as limit amount and expiry date, and creating sub limits if needed. It also explains the different types of limits and product variants available.


Raghuram | 14 steps | 2 minutes


Click on +New limit to create limits.
Limit: sanctioned by the FI, based on the available limit disbursements occur.

Select the customer for the list of customers.
Pre-requisite: Complete Customer Setup to create limits.

Choose the customer from the list.

Click continue to add more limits.

Click on the icon to enter further details.

Enter the mandatory details - limit amount, operative amount, and expiry date.
Limit Amount - the total amount that is sanctioned by the FI.
Operative Limit - the amount the FI will disburse to the customer based on their credit history.

7 Select the expiry date from the calendar.

Enter Ad hoc limit, if applicable.
Ad hoc Limit: limit over and above the normal limit.
FI decides whether to allocate this based on credit worthiness.

Click on save.

To create sub limits, select + sign. To view the information fed, click on the eye icon.
Sub limits can be same as parent limit but cannot be greater.

Choose the limit level.
1. Aggregate - combination of limits (cannot exceed parent limit)
2.Non-aggregate - limit of each product variant.
Select either of the two.

Select the Product Nature from list of value.
Revolving - It refers to a flexible line of credit that can be used repeatedly. Non Revolving - It is a fixed amount of credit that cannot be replenished once it is utilized. Choose the option that applies.

Select the product from the list.

Fill the other remaining details and click on save.
Create as many sub limits as required.
Once the entire information is filled, it will go to the checker for approval. After Approval, the limit will reflect on the main screen.
